Rachel Carson

Rachel Carson (American Marine Biologist/Writer/Ecologist) was born May 27, 1907 and died Apr. 14 1964. Some neat facts about her journey as a marine biologist:

  • Wrote the popular book, Silent Spring, where it questioned modern science and its direction and scope, warned about misuse of chemical pesticides to natural systems, and started the whole “contemporary environmental movement.”
  • One of the pioneers of the ecologist/“Save the Environment” movement.
  • Talked about climate change before it was even mentioned!
  • In Carson’s books, she used submarine technology and underwater research to write about her geologic discoveries - like how islands were formed, how ocean currents change, how temperature affects sea life, etc.
