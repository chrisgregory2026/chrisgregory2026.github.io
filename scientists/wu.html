Chien-Shiung Wu

Chien-Shiung Wu (Chinese-American Physicist) was born May 31, 1912 and died Feb. 16, 1997. Below are some interesting facts about her life!

  • Chien-Shiung helped develop the process of separating uranium into U-245 and U-238 isotopes (essential for nuclear fission reactors today) by gasseous diffusion.
  • Helped improve Geiger counters (which are the tools used for measuring radiation levels).
  • The only Chinese person (and woman) to have worked on the Manhattan Project!
  • Has been awarded the Comstock Prize and Wolf Prize in Physics!
