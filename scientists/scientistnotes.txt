Katherine Johnson
American Mathematician
Born Aug. 26, 1918
Died Feb. 24, 2020
	* Was the first woman in the Flight Research Division (FRD) to receive credit in a research report as an author.
	* Has done the orbital equations for a computer... BY HAND! Only because the astronauts were skeptical of the computer's results.
	* Was with NASA (NACA back then) since June 1953 and retired 1986.
	* Integrated as one of three black students at West Virginia's graduate schools.

Bessie Coleman
American Aviator
Born 1892
Died 1926
	* Was the first African American AND the first Native American woman pilot.
	* Performed flying tricks and was nicknamed "Brave Bessie," "Queen Bess," and "The Only Race Aviatrix in the World."
	* Survived a major airplane accident when, in Feb. 1923, her plane suddenly became unfunctional and crashed. She had a broken leg, cracked ribs, and cuts on her face, yet she fully healed from that accident.
	* However, when flying with a mechanic William Wills, a wrench lodged into the engine of the plane and Wills loss control. The plane flipped upside down releasing Bessie (planes back then had no roofs or protection) into her saddening death, and soon after, Wills crashed next to her.

Rachel Carson
American Marine Biologist/Writer/Ecologist
Born 1907
Died 1964
	* Wrote the popular book, Silent Spring, where it questioned modern science and its direction and scope, warned about misuse of chemical pesticides to natural systems, and started the whole "contemporary environmental movement."
	* One of the pioneers of the ecologist/environment movement.
	* Talked about climate change before it was even mentioned in "science!"
	* In Carson's books, she used submarine technology and underwater research and wrote about geologic discoveries - like how islands were formed, how ocean currents change, how temperature affects sea life, etc.

Chien-Shiung Wu
Chinese-American Physicist
Born May 31, 1912
Died Feb. 16, 1997
	* Helped develop the process of separating uranium into U-235 and U-238 (essential for nuclear fission) by gasseous diffusion.
	* Helped improved Geiger counters (for measuring radiation levels).
	* The only Chinese person to have worked on the Manhattan Project!
	* Awarded the Comstock Prize and Wolf Prize in Physics!
