Bessie Coleman

Bessie Coleman (American Aviator) was born in Jan. 26, 1892 and died tragically on Apr. 30, 1926. Down below are some thrilling facts before the accident:

  • Was the first African American AND the first Native American woman pilot.
  • Performed flying tricks and was nicknamed “Brave Bessie,” “Queen Bess,” “The Only Race Aviatrix in the World.”
  • Survived a major airplane accident when, in Feb. 1923, her plane suddenly became unfunctional and crashed. She had a broken leg, cracked ribs, and cuts on her face, yet she fully healed!
  • However, when flying with a mechanic William Wills, a wrench lodged into the engine of the plane and Wills lost control. The plane flipped upside down releasing Bessie (because planes back then had no roofs or protection) into her saddening death, and soon after, Wills crashed next to her and died.
